F A C T S.

The United States of America’s criminal justice system is broken, and has been broken for as long as we could remember.
With hate crimes across the nation, people of color, including African Americans and Latinos, are of the first to be targeted by police officers.
Among them is the country they live in, the United States of America.
With the most prisoners in the world, accounting to 25% of the world’s population, here are some statistics on criminal injustice in the United States...

  • Of all crimes that happen in our country, only ten percent of them are reported.
  • More African-Americans are in the prison system than there were slaves in the 1850s.
  • One in fifteen African-American men are behind bars.
  • One in thirteen African-Americans are no longer able to vote, being that they were once imprisoned.
  • The “War on Drugs” has mostly affected those of color, imprisoning more Hispanic and African-American drug users compared to their Caucasian counterparts.
  • People of color are more likely to be searched during traffic stops, with Hispanics being searched 7% of the time and African-Americans being searched 6% of the time.
  • Black youth is twice as likely to be arrested for crimes in school compared to the white youth.
  • African-Americans are more likely to be sentenced to serious prison time, whereas their Caucasian counterparts do community service.
  • African-Americans are more likely to be detained and to wait for a trial compared to whites.
  • One in six Latino men are expected to be imprisoned once in their lifetime in the United States.
  • 1 in 111 white women are expected to be imprisoned once in their lifetime in the United States, compared to 1 in 18 for black women.
  • It costs nearly $100,000+ to incarcerate a single person, every year, with US taxpayers paying nearly $70 billion a year to maintain the prison system.
  • Nearly 77% of prisoners are rearrested within five years in the United States, having one of the highest recidivism rates.
